Best Swim Caps for Every Swimmer

Protect your hair from chlorine damage, reduce drag in the water, and keep hair out of your face. This guide covers silicone vs. latex, caps for long hair, and how to find the perfect fit for adults and kids.

Why Wear a Swim Cap?

Swim caps aren't just for competitive swimmers. They offer real benefits for anyone who spends time in the poolfrom protecting your hair to improving safety and comfort.

  • Protects hair from chlorine: Chlorine strips natural oils, causes dryness, and can even discolor treated or bleached hair. A cap creates a barrier that reduces chemical exposure.
  • Reduces drag: Streamlined head profile means less resistance in the waterespecially important for competitive swimmers and triathletes.
  • Keeps hair out of your face: No more mid-lap adjustments. A cap keeps hair secure so you can focus on your stroke and breathing.
  • Pool hygiene: Caps reduce the amount of loose hair, product residue, and oils entering pool watermany public pools require them for this reason.
  • Better goggle seal: Wearing a cap over goggle straps prevents snagging and keeps goggles secure, especially during dive starts.
Pro tip: Wet your hair before putting on a swim cap. This reduces friction, makes the cap easier to put on, and helps create a better seal around the edges.

Silicone vs. Latex vs. Lycra: Which Is Best?

Not all swim caps are made equal. Here's how the three main materials compare.

Most popular

Silicone

The best all-around choice. Durable, comfortable, hypoallergenic, and easy to put on. Stretches well without tearing. Typically lasts 1-2 years with regular use. Costs $8-$20.

Budget option

Latex

Thin, lightweight, and cheap ($3-$8). Great for competitive swimmers who replace caps frequently. Downside: less durable, can cause allergic reactions, and tears more easily than silicone.

Recreational

Lycra / Fabric

Breathable and comfortable, but offers zero waterproofing. Best for aqua aerobics, water polo practice, or keeping hair back during casual swimming. Not suitable for protecting hair from chlorine.

Best Swim Caps for Long Hair

Standard swim caps often don't fit comfortably over long, thick, or voluminous hair. Here's what works better.

  • Extra-large silicone caps: Designed specifically for long hair with more room in the crown. Brands like Speedo and TYR offer "long hair" versions with extended dimensions.
  • Double-cap technique: Competitive swimmers often wear two capsone to tuck the hair, a second on top for compression and a sleek finish. This is the gold standard for maximum protection and hydrodynamics.
  • Tucking tips: Flip your head upside down, gather hair into a low bun or ponytail, then pull the cap from front to back. Tuck stray hairs last. Practice makes this quick and easy.
  • Avoid latex for long hair: Latex doesn't stretch as much as silicone and is more likely to snag or tear when pulling over thick hair. Stick with silicone for comfort and durability.
For natural and textured hair: The Soul Cap and FINIS brands make extra-large caps designed for natural Black hair, locs, braids, and weaves. These caps provide significantly more room than standard designs and have been approved for competitive use by FINA.

Swim Caps for Kids

Getting a swim cap on a squirmy child takes patiencebut the right cap and technique make it much easier.

  • Youth sizing: Kids' caps are smaller in diameter and designed for smaller heads. Adult caps will slip off or feel uncomfortably tight.
  • Fun designs: Bright colors, patterns, and characters make kids more willing to wear them. Let your child pick their own capownership increases cooperation.
  • How to put one on without tears: Place both hands inside the cap to stretch it open wide, position it on the forehead, then pull back over the head in one smooth motion. Never yank or pull hair.
  • Silicone over latex for kids: Silicone is gentler, less likely to pull hair, and easier for small hands to manage. It's worth the extra few dollars.
Parent tip: Practice at home before the first swim lesson. Let kids try putting the cap on themselves in front of a mirror. Familiarity reduces stress and makes pool time smoother.

Care & Maintenance

A little care extends the life of your swim cap significantlyhere's how to make it last.

After every swim

Rinse in fresh water

Chlorine degrades silicone and latex over time. A quick rinse in cold tap water removes chemicals and prolongs the life of your cap.

Drying

Dry flat, not folded

Hang or lay your cap flat to dry. Avoid folding it while wet, which can cause creases and weak spots that lead to tearing.

Storage

Sprinkle with talcum powder

Dust the inside of silicone and latex caps with talc or cornstarch to prevent them from sticking together. This makes them easier to put on and prevents tearing during storage.

When to replace: Swim caps typically last 3-6 months with regular use (2-3 times per week). Replace your cap if you notice thinning material, small tears, loss of elasticity, or if it no longer creates a snug seal.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do swim caps keep hair dry?

No swim cap is 100% waterproof. Silicone and latex caps reduce water exposure significantly, but some moisture will seep in around the edges. For best protection, tuck all hair inside, wear a tight fit, and consider a double-cap technique for very long hair.

What size swim cap do I need?

Most swim caps come in standard adult and youth sizes. Adults with very long or thick hair should look for extra-large or long-hair specific caps. Kids under age 10 typically need youth-sized caps. Silicone caps stretch more than latex, offering a more forgiving fit.

Are swim caps required at pools?

Many public pools require swim caps to reduce hair and debris in the filtration system, improve hygiene, and minimize clogged drains. Competitive swimming and water polo always require caps. Check your pool's rulesmany provide disposable caps if you forget yours.
