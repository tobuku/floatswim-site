Filters (Future-Ready)

These controls will eventually let you explore incidents by age group, location, water type, and timeframe. For now, they show the intended structure and language of the dashboard.

Planned filters:
  • Age group (Infant, 1–4, 5–12, Teen, Adult)
  • Water type (Pool, Ocean, Lake/River, Home/Bath, Other)
  • Region (State, metro, or zip-based)
  • Timeframe (Today, last 7 days, last 30 days, year-to-date)
This prototype is intentionally non-interactive and anonymous. Real data will be handled with care, especially incidents involving children, and will avoid identifiable private information.

Map & Incident List (Concept Preview)

Below is a concept layout for a future map and incident listing. No real incidents are shown here; everything is a structural placeholder to guide design and development.

Map area (future):

This section will host an interactive map with:

  • Pins representing incidents within the selected timeframe.
  • Color or icon variations for drowning vs. near-drowning.
  • High-level summaries when hovering or tapping on a region.
  • Zoom controls to switch from national to state-level detail.
Incident list (example format):

A filterable list without names, focusing on:

  • Date and approximate time of incident.
  • Age bracket (e.g., “2–4 years”).
  • Setting (e.g., “Residential pool,” “Lake,” “Bathtub”).
  • Outcome (e.g., “Survived (hospitalized),” “Fatal”).

The goal: communicate patterns respectfully, without sensationalism or unnecessary identifying detail.

Respect, Privacy, and Purpose

Any data visualized here in the future will be guided by a simple principle: serve prevention and healing, not curiosity or clicks.

  • No graphic imagery or invasive personal details.
  • Emphasis on patterns, risk factors, and prevention opportunities.
  • Clear disclaimers about data sources and limitations.
As FloatSwim develops, the dashboard may be reviewed by pediatric, legal, and data privacy advisors to ensure that the way incidents are represented is both accurate and respectful.
